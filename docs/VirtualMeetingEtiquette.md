# Topic 7: Virtual Meeting Etiquette

> **Manager Mindset:** A productive virtual meeting is not about talking more—it's about ensuring everyone understands, participates, and leaves with clear action items.

----------

# 1. What is Virtual Meeting Etiquette?

**Definition**

Virtual meeting etiquette is the set of professional behaviors that make online meetings **effective, respectful, and productive**.

### Why It Matters

-   Improves collaboration
-   Increases participation
-   Reduces misunderstandings
-   Saves time
-   Creates accountability

----------

# 2. Where You Use It (Manager Perspective)


## Meeting Best Practices by Situation

| **Situation** | **Best Practice** |
|---|---|
| **👨‍💻 Daily Stand-up** | Keep updates short and discuss blockers. |
| **🏃 Sprint Planning** | Share the agenda and confirm everyone's understanding. |
| **🔄 Sprint Review** | Encourage stakeholder feedback and answer questions openly. |
| **🔁 Sprint Retrospective** | Give everyone an opportunity to share improvements and lessons learned. |
| **👨‍💼 1:1 Meetings** | Give your full attention and avoid multitasking. |
| **🤝 Client Calls** | Be punctual, professional, and well prepared. |
| **📊 Stakeholder Meetings** | Present concise status updates, risks, and action items. |
----------

# 3. Best Practices Before the Meeting

-   ✅ Join 2–5 minutes early.
-   ✅ Test microphone, camera, and internet.
-   ✅ Share the meeting agenda.
-   ✅ Review required documents.
-   ✅ Join from a quiet environment.

----------

# 4. Best Practices During the Meeting

-   Keep your microphone muted when not speaking.
-   Maintain eye contact with the camera.
-   Avoid multitasking.
-   Listen actively.
-   Stay on the agenda.
-   Encourage participation.
-   Ask clarifying questions.
-   Respect others while speaking.

----------

# 5. Best Practices After the Meeting

-   Share meeting notes (MoM).
-   Assign action items.
-   Mention owners and deadlines.
-   Confirm next steps.

----------

# 6. Creating an Inclusive Environment

This question appeared in your assessment.

### What does an inclusive virtual meeting look like?

-   Give everyone an opportunity to speak.
-   Encourage quieter members to share ideas.
-   Listen without interrupting.
-   Respect different opinions.
-   Appreciate contributions.

### Manager Example

Instead of asking:

> "Anyone has questions?"

Ask:

> "Rahul, you worked on this module. Do you have any suggestions?"

This encourages participation.

# 7. Practical Examples

## 👨‍💻 Daily Stand-up

✅ Share:

-   Yesterday's work
-   Today's plan
-   Blockers

Avoid discussing detailed technical solutions.

----------

## 🏃 Sprint Planning

-   Share the sprint goal.
-   Explain priorities.
-   Confirm story understanding.
-   Encourage questions.

----------

## 🔄 Sprint Review

-   Demonstrate completed work.
-   Invite stakeholder feedback.
-   Avoid becoming defensive.

----------

## 🔁 Sprint Retrospective

-   Allow everyone to speak.
-   Focus on process improvements.
-   Don't blame individuals.

----------

## 🤝 Client Calls

-   Join on time.
-   Introduce participants.
-   Speak clearly.
-   Summarize agreed actions.

----------

## 👨‍💼 1:1 Meetings

-   Turn on the camera when appropriate.
-   Give full attention.
-   Listen more than you speak.

----------

# 8. Common Mistakes


## Common Meeting Mistakes and Better Approaches

| **Mistake** | **Better Approach** |
|---|---|
| **Joining late** | **Join early and test your equipment.** |
| **No agenda** | **Share the agenda before the meeting.** |
| **Talking over others** | **Wait for your turn and let others finish speaking.** |
| **Multitasking** | **Stay fully engaged and give the meeting your full attention.** |
| **Ending without action items** | **Summarize decisions and assign action items with owners and deadlines.** |
| **Ignoring quiet participants** | **Invite everyone to contribute and encourage participation.** |

# 9. Scenario Questions

### Scenario 1

The meeting has no agenda, and discussions keep changing topics.

**What should the manager improve?**

✅ Share a clear agenda and keep discussions focused.

----------

### Scenario 2

Only two people speak during Sprint Review.

**How can the manager improve engagement?**

✅ Invite quieter team members to share their opinions.

----------

### Scenario 3

A manager ends the meeting without assigning owners or deadlines.

**What is missing?**

✅ Action items and next steps.

----------

### Scenario 4

A participant keeps interrupting others.

**What should the manager do?**

✅ Politely moderate the discussion and ensure everyone gets a chance to speak.

# 12. Quick Revision


## Meeting Etiquette: Do's and Don'ts

| **Do ✅** | **Don't ❌** |
|---|---|
| **Join early** | **Join late.** |
| **Share the agenda** | **Start the meeting without a plan.** |
| **Encourage participation** | **Let only a few people speak.** |
| **Stay focused** | **Go off-topic.** |
| **Assign action items** | **End the meeting without defining next steps.** |

# 🔑 Manager Takeaways

### 👨‍💻 Daily Stand-up

-   Keep updates short.
-   Focus on blockers.

### 🏃 Sprint Planning

-   Explain goals clearly.
-   Confirm everyone understands.

### 🔄 Sprint Review

-   Encourage stakeholder feedback.
-   Invite quieter team members to speak.

### 🔁 Sprint Retrospective

-   Listen to improvement ideas.
-   Focus on process, not blame.

### 🤝 Client Calls

-   Be punctual.
-   Summarize decisions and expectations.

### 👨‍💼 1:1 Meetings

-   Give full attention.
-   Build trust through active listening.

**Golden Rule:**  
**Every virtual meeting should end with three things:**

1.  ✅ Clear decisions
2.  ✅ Action items
3.  ✅ Owner + Deadline
