# Topic 6: Body Language & Gestures

> **Manager Mindset:** People don't just listen to your words—they observe your **body language, facial expressions, gestures, eye contact, and tone of voice**. Your non-verbal communication should support your message.

----------

# 1. What is Body Language?

**Definition**

Body language is **non-verbal communication** expressed through:

-   Facial expressions
-   Eye contact
-   Hand gestures
-   Posture
-   Voice tone
-   Personal space

> **Communication = Words + Tone + Body Language**

----------

# 2. Why It Matters (Manager Perspective)

Good body language helps you:

-   Build trust
-   Show confidence
-   Improve leadership presence
-   Keep meetings engaging
-   Resolve conflicts calmly

Poor body language can make people think you are:

-   Angry
-   Disinterested
-   Unapproachable
-   Nervous

----------

# 3. Where You Use It (Manager Perspective)


## Good Body Language in Different Situations

| **Situation** | **Good Body Language** |
|---|---|
| **👨‍💻 Daily Stand-up** | Maintain eye contact and stand confidently. |
| **🏃 Sprint Planning** | Listen attentively and avoid checking your phone. |
| **🔄 Sprint Review** | Smile appropriately and acknowledge feedback positively. |
| **👨‍💼 1:1 Meetings** | Face the employee and maintain an open posture. |
| **💻 Code Review** | Stay calm and avoid negative facial expressions. |
| **🤝 Client Calls** | Look at the camera, smile naturally, and use confident gestures. |
| **🎤 Interviews** | Sit straight, maintain eye contact, and speak confidently. |
| **⚠️ Conflict Resolution** | Stay calm and avoid pointing fingers or crossing your arms. |

----------

# 4. Important Body Language Elements


## Elements of Effective Body Language

| **Element** | **Best Practice** |
|---|---|
| **Eye Contact** | Shows confidence, interest, and attention. |
| **Facial Expressions** | Match your message (smile when appropriate). |
| **Hand Gestures** | Use natural gestures and avoid excessive movement. |
| **Posture** | Sit or stand upright to project confidence and professionalism. |
| **Voice Tone** | Speak clearly, calmly, and at an appropriate pace. |
| **Personal Space** | Respect others' comfort and maintain appropriate distance. |
----------

# 5. Practical Examples

## 👨‍💻 During Daily Stand-up

❌ Looking at your laptop while others speak.

✅ Look at the speaker and acknowledge them.

----------

## 👨‍💼 During 1:1 Meetings

❌ Fold your arms and frequently check your phone.

✅ Maintain eye contact and an open posture.

----------

## 🤝 During Client Calls

❌ Looking away from the screen.

✅ Look into the camera to create better engagement.

----------

## 💻 During Code Reviews

❌ Rolling your eyes or showing frustration.

✅ Stay neutral and discuss the code respectfully.

----------

## 🔄 During Sprint Review

When stakeholders give feedback:

❌ Defensive expression.

✅ Listen, nod, and thank them for the feedback.

----------

# 6. Mirror Practice

One of the best techniques to improve:

-   Pronunciation
-   Confidence
-   Facial expressions
-   Eye contact
-   Hand gestures

### Practice

-   Speak for 2–3 minutes daily.
-   Observe your posture and expressions.
-   Reduce unnecessary gestures.
-   Smile naturally.

----------

# 7. Common Mistakes


## Common Body Language Mistakes and Better Approaches

| **Mistake** | **Better Approach** |
|---|---|
| **No eye contact** | **Maintain natural eye contact.** |
| **Crossing arms** | **Keep an open and relaxed posture.** |
| **Looking at your phone** | **Give the speaker your full attention.** |
| **Speaking too fast** | **Slow down and speak clearly.** |
| **Excessive hand movements** | **Use controlled and natural gestures.** |
| **Frowning while listening** | **Maintain a neutral or positive facial expression.** |

----------

# 8. Scenario Questions

### Scenario 1

During a client meeting, the manager keeps looking at another screen while speaking.

**Impact?**

✅ It appears the manager is distracted or not interested.

----------

### Scenario 2

A developer is explaining an issue.

The manager nods, maintains eye contact, and listens without interrupting.

**What does this demonstrate?**

✅ Positive body language and active listening.

----------

### Scenario 3

During a presentation, the speaker keeps their hands in their pockets and avoids eye contact.

**What should they improve?**

✅ Eye contact, posture, and natural hand gestures.

----------

### Scenario 4

A manager gives feedback with crossed arms and an angry expression.

**How might the employee feel?**

✅ Defensive, uncomfortable, and less open to feedback.

# 10. Interview Questions

### Q1. Why is body language important for managers?

**Answer:**  
Body language builds trust, improves confidence, reinforces spoken messages, and helps create positive relationships with team members and stakeholders.

----------

### Q2. How can you improve your body language?

**Answer:**

-   Maintain eye contact.
-   Sit or stand with good posture.
-   Use natural hand gestures.
-   Smile when appropriate.
-   Practice speaking in front of a mirror.
-   Avoid distracting habits like checking your phone.

----------

# 11. Quick Revision


## Body Language: Do's and Don'ts

| **Do ✅** | **Don't ❌** |
|---|---|
| **Maintain eye contact** | **Avoid eye contact.** |
| **Sit or stand confidently** | **Slouch or appear disengaged.** |
| **Use natural gestures** | **Overuse hand movements or fidget excessively.** |
| **Smile appropriately** | **Show frustration or negative expressions unnecessarily.** |
| **Look at the camera during virtual meetings** | **Look away constantly or get distracted during online meetings.** |
----------

# 🔑 Manager Takeaways

### During Daily Stand-up

-   Give full attention.
-   Avoid multitasking.

### During Client Calls

-   Look at the camera.
-   Maintain a confident posture.

### During 1:1 Meetings

-   Listen with an open posture.
-   Avoid distractions.

### During Feedback Sessions

-   Stay calm.
-   Use positive facial expressions.
-   Never let negative body language undermine your message.

**Golden Rule:**  
**Your body language should reinforce your words, not contradict them.**
